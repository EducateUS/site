SAT® Writing Tips

The SAT® Writing section has questions that follow patterns. Practice is very helpful in recognizing this. Additionally, here are some specific content notes to remember:

  • Ignore prepositional phrases
    • e.g. Each of the men is working. “Of the men” is irrelevant here, “each” is all that matters for agreement)
  • Its' is not a word
  • Semicolons separate two independent clauses. If the sentence would't stand on its own, you can't use a semicolon. Usually, a semicolon is used instead of a period if the two sentences are very closely connected in subject.
  • Colons are used for expanded definitions or to introduce a list. They must be preceded by an independent clause.

One other thing to note: the shortest answer is frequently the correct one. Don't take this too far, but it may be helpful for guessing.
